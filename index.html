The Problem
Military readiness tracking was broken.
📋
Problem 01
Manual & Error-Prone
Squadron leaders tracked personnel in spreadsheets — no live updates, high risk of outdated or overwritten data with every change.
👁️
Problem 02
No Readiness Visibility
Commanders had no instant view of available vs. TDY, medical, or deployed. They had to count it manually, every time.
🔓
Problem 03
No Access Control
Shared files gave everyone equal edit rights. No role separation — one wrong click could overwrite critical manning data.
45 min

Twice a week — over 70 hours of leadership time lost per year, per squadron.
Background & Literature
Grounded in doctrine and research.
Operational Context
Military personnel readiness tracking is a mission-critical function. Incorrect or stale manning data directly affects sortie generation, weapons load planning, and force protection decisions.

Existing DoD solutions (MICT, LIMS-EV) are network-dependent, slow to update, and provide no visual drag-and-drop interface for rapid reassignment.

SMRVT was designed to fill the gap at the squadron level — where decisions happen quickly and network access is not always guaranteed.
Supporting Literature
Offline-First Design
PouchDB / CouchDB replication (Apache, 2023) — persistent local storage with optional remote sync, meeting DoD self-contained environment requirements.
Access Control
NIST SP 800-53 AC-2 / AC-3 — least-privilege principles enforced by restricting structural edits to authorized admin accounts only.
Readiness Metrics
AFI 10-201 (Force Readiness Reporting) — defines how TDY, Medical, Leave, and Deployed statuses are excluded from unit readiness calculations.
Design & Development Approach
Five phases. One working system.
01
Requirements
Gathered from USAF munitions unit workflows. Defined role separation, offline-first capability, and readiness logic.
02
Architecture
Modular ES6 split: main.js · state.js · config.js · dragDrop.js · metrics.js · importExport.js
03
Development
Iterative 5-person build. PouchDB persistence, drag-and-drop swap logic, 20-step undo stack.
04
Integration
PouchDB ↔ IndexedDB local. Optional CouchDB / Cloudant bidirectional replication for multi-user sync.
05
Testing
10 structured test cases covering functional, usability, validation, integration, regression. All passed.
Key Features
Every feature maps to a stated requirement.
🖱️
Drag & Drop Assignment
Move personnel between section slots with automatic swap logic and instant readiness recalculation.
🔒
Role-Based Access Control
Admin and User accounts with session persistence. Structural edits restricted to authorized admin only.
💾
Offline-First PouchDB
All data persists locally in IndexedDB. No server or internet connection required during operation.
📊
Live Readiness Metrics
Centralized metrics engine calculates manning and readiness percentage across all sections in real time.
☁️
Remote CouchDB Sync
Optional bidirectional sync with CouchDB or IBM Cloudant — live updates across multiple clients.
📄
CSV & PDF Export
Full roster CSV export for backup and re-import. Landscape PDF with metrics and full personnel summary.
